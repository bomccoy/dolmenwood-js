# Kindreds

## Breggle

Goat-headed folk whose horn length indicates their social standing.

- **Kindred Type**: Mortal
- **Level 1 PC Age**: 15 + 2d10 years
- **Lifespan**: 50 + 2d20 years
- **Height**: 5′4″ + 2d6″ (Medium)
- **Weight**: 120 + 6d10 lbs
- **Native Languages**: Woldish, Gaffe, Caprice

### Traits

**Fur**: +1 AC when unarmoured or wearing Light armour.

**Horns**: Breggles may make a melee attack with their horns instead of a weapon. The damage inflicted increases with Level. A breggle character begins play as a commoner of the shorthorn caste. As they advance in Level, gaining experience, wealth, and renown, their horns grow. Upon reaching Level 4, a breggle character's horns have grown to the point that the character is recognised as a longhorn.

**Gaze (Level 4+)**: The longhorn gazes intently at an individual human or shorthorn. If the target fails a **Save Versus Spell**, they are charmed to view the longhorn with awe and respect; while charmed, the target is unable to harm the longhorn, either directly or indirectly. Duration: until next sunrise. A longhorn may use their gaze a limited number of times per day depending on their Level (see Breggle Advancement). The gaze may be used on a specific subject at most once per day. The holy spell *Mantle of Protection* counters a longhorn's gaze.

## Elf

Ageless fairies who have crossed into the mortal world for reasons they seldom reveal.

- **Kindred Type**: Fairy
- **Level 1 PC Age**: 1d100 × 10 years
- **Lifespan**: Immortal
- **Height**: 5′ + 2d6″ (Medium)
- **Weight**: 100 + 3d10 lbs
- **Native Languages**: Woldish, Sylvan, High Elfish

### Traits

**Glamours**: Possesses a single randomly determined glamour.

**Immortality**: Cannot die naturally; immune to non-magical diseases. Cannot die from thirst or starvation, but lack of sustenance drives the elf desperate and sadistic.

**Magic Resistance**: +2 Magic Resistance.

**Skills**: Skill Target of 5 for Listen and Search.

**Unearthly Beauty**: +2 bonus to Charisma (maximum 18) when interacting with mortals.

**Vulnerable to Cold Iron**: Cold iron weapons inflict +1 damage.

## Grimalkin

Mercurial feline fairies who shift between three different forms.

- **Kindred Type**: Fairy
- **Level 1 PC Age**: 1d100 × 10 years
- **Lifespan**: Immortal
- **Height**: 2′6″ + 2d6″ (Small)
- **Weight**: 50 + 3d10 lbs
- **Native Languages**: Woldish, Mewl

### Traits

**Armour and Weapons**: Armour must be tailored to small size. Cannot wield Large weapons.

**Defensive Bonus**: +2 AC in melee with Large creatures, due to small size.

**Eating Giant Rodents**: After spending 1 Turn devouring a freshly killed giant rodent, heals 1 HP.

**Glamours**: Possesses a single randomly determined glamour.

**Immortality**: Cannot die naturally; immune to non-magical diseases. Cannot die from thirst or starvation, but lack of sustenance drives them desperate and sadistic.

**Magic Resistance**: +2 Magic Resistance.

**Shape-Shifting**: Can transform (1 Round) into either a fat domestic cat (*chester*) or a primal fey form (*wilder*). Cannot use weapons, magic, or class abilities while transformed. All gear optionally transforms with the grimalkin.

*Chester (unlimited):*
- AC 12, Speed 30; Bite + two claw attacks each Round; each attack inflicts 1 damage
- Can understand language; responds only in yowls and meows
- Changing back: 1 Round; only possible when unobserved by any sentient being

*Wilder (once per day):*
- May only enter when in melee and below half maximum HP; heals 2d6 HP on entering
- Near-invisible: only eyes visible; opponents suffer −2 to Attack Rolls
- AC 13, Speed 30; Bite + two claws each Round, +2 to Attack Rolls; each attack inflicts 1d4 damage
- Fey chaos: cannot distinguish friend from foe; attacks nearest creature
- Returns to humanoid form after 2d4 Rounds

**Skills**: Skill Target of 5 for Listen.

**Vulnerable to Cold Iron**: Cold iron weapons inflict +1 damage.

## Human

The most widespread kindred in Dolmenwood.

- **Kindred Type**: Mortal
- **Level 1 PC Age**: 15 + 2d10 years
- **Lifespan**: 50 + 2d20 years
- **Height**: Male: 5′4″ + 2d6″ (Medium), Female: 5′ + 2d6″ (Medium)
- **Weight**: 120 + 6d10 lbs
- **Native Languages**: Woldish

### Traits

**Class Freedom**: Humans may choose any of the 9 classes.

**DECISIVENESS**: When an Initiative Roll is tied, humans act first.

**LEADERSHIP**: The Loyalty rating of retainers in the employ of a human character is increased by 1.

**SPIRITED**: +10% bonus to all Experience Points earned.

## Mossling

Gnarled, woody humanoids whose fertile flesh hosts mosses, moulds, and fungi.

- **Kindred Type**: Mortal
- **Level 1 PC Age**: 50 + 3d6 years
- **Lifespan**: 200 + 5d8 × 10 years
- **Height**: 3′6″ + 2d6″ (Small)
- **Weight**: 150 + 2d20 lbs
- **Native Languages**: Woldish, Mulch

### Traits

**Armour and Weapons**: Armour must be tailored to small size. Cannot wield Large weapons. Mosslings use bark armour in place of chainmail, and pinecone armour in place of plate mail.

**Knacks**: Each mossling knows one knack, rolled or chosen at character creation. See Mossling Knacks.

**Resilience**: +4 bonus to Saving Throws against fungal spores and poisons; +2 bonus to all other Saving Throws.

**Skills**: Skill Target of 5 for Survival when foraging.

**Symbiotic Flesh**: At each Level (including Level 1), the mossling acquires a random trait from the Symbiotic Flesh table (d20). Duplicates may be re-rolled or treated as an amplification.

## Woodgrue

Bat-faced demi-fey goblins, known for their love of music, revelry, and arson.

- **Kindred Type**: Demi-fey
- **Level 1 PC Age**: 50 + 3d6 years
- **Lifespan**: 300 + 2d100 years
- **Height**: 3′ + 2d6″ (Small)
- **Weight**: 60 + 2d10 lbs
- **Native Languages**: Woldish, Sylvan

### Traits

**Armour and Weapons**: Armour must be tailored to small size. Cannot wield Large weapons.

**Compulsive Jubilation**: When a woodgrue witnesses a party, feast, celebration, or festival, they are utterly compelled to partake. May **Save Versus Spell** to resist; on failure, they feel drained and downtrodden for the length of the engagement.

**Defensive Bonus**: +2 AC in melee with Large creatures, due to small size.

**Mad Revelry**: Once per day, the woodgrue may play one of the enchanted melodies below on a wind instrument while hooting and dancing terribly. All living creatures within 30′ (including allies) must **Save Versus Spell** or be afflicted; fairies and demi-fey gain a +2 bonus to this save. Effects last while the woodgrue keeps playing. The woodgrue may move but cannot attack or perform other actions. If the woodgrue is harmed or fails a Saving Throw, the melody ends; affected creatures who are also harmed or fail a Saving Throw cease to be affected.

*Enchanted Melodies:*
- **Confide**: Subjects speak in a slurred voice, confessing a hidden emotion or revealing an ally's secret
- **Dance**: Subjects begin dancing; +1 AC bonus but cannot move from their spot
- **Imbibe**: Subjects ravenously consume any available liquids, herbs, and mushrooms; act as though drunk (−2 to Attack Rolls)
- **Jape**: Subjects mock the immediately preceding occurrence
- **Jubilate**: Subjects burst into laughter; 1-in-6 chance each Round of falling over
- **Mount**: Subjects attempt to mount nearby creatures. **Save Versus Hold** to resist. Unaffected creatures **Save Versus Hold** each Round to buck off a rider
- **Revel**: Subjects cannot speak; instead bark scats of sound. Speed halved if not headed toward the woodgrue

**Moon Sight**: Can see in darkness up to 60′, as if in faint moonlight. No low-light penalties; fine detail (e.g. writing) cannot be perceived.

**Musical Instruments**: Can employ a musical instrument as a melee weapon, dealing 1d4 damage.

**Starting Equipment**: Begins play with a wind instrument.

**Vulnerable to Cold Iron**: Cold iron weapons inflict +1 damage.
