# Classes

## Bard

- **Prime Abilities**: CHA, DEX
- **HP**: 1d6 per Level; +1 after Level 10
- **Combat Aptitude**: Semi-martial
- **Armour**: Light and Medium; no shields
- **Weapons**: Small and Medium

### Starting Equipment
- Armour (roll 1d6): 1–2. None  3–4. Leather  5–6. Chainmail
- Weapons (roll 1d6 twice): 1. Club  2. 3 daggers  3. Longsword  4. Sling + 20 stones  5. Shortbow + 20 arrows  6. Shortsword
- Class items: Musical instrument (stringed or wind)

### Class Abilities

**Bard Skills**: Skill Target of 5 for Listen, plus three specialised skills: Decipher Document, Legerdemain, and Monster Lore. Skill Targets improve with Level (see Bard Skill Targets).

- **Decipher Document**: Understand the gist of a non-magical text in an unknown language, unravel a cipher, or identify cryptically labelled landmarks on a map. May only retry after gaining a Level.
- **Legerdemain**: Sleight of hand — palm a small object, slip a poison into a drink, or pilfer an item. −1 penalty per 3 Levels of the victim or observer. On a natural 1, Save Versus Doom or be noticed.
- **Monster Lore**: Identify a monster and its basic powers and vulnerabilities based on myth and folklore. May only retry after gaining a Level.

**Counter Charm**: While the bard plays music and sings, allies within 30′ are immune to magical effects based on music or song and gain a +2 bonus to Saving Throws against fairy magic. Usable once per Turn. The bard may move at half Speed while playing but cannot attack or perform other actions. Ends immediately if the bard is harmed or fails a Saving Throw.

**Enchantment**: By playing music and singing, the bard can fascinate subjects in a 30′ radius. Usable once per day per Level; cannot be used in combat. The bard may move at half Speed but cannot attack. Targets whose total Levels equal up to twice the bard's Level may be affected. At Level 1 only mortals can be affected; from Level 4, animals and demi-fey as well; from Level 7, fairies and monstrosities as well. Each subject must Save Versus Spell or be fascinated — rapt attention on the performance, following the bard if they move. Ends immediately on interruption (loud noise, violence). If the performance lasted at least 1 Turn and ended without interruption, each fascinated subject must Save Versus Spell again (+2 bonus) or be charmed: regarded as a trusted friend, obeying commands (if a shared language exists), but may resist commands that contradict their Alignment or that are suicidal. Duration: 1 Turn per Level.

## Cleric

- **Prime Abilities**: WIS
- **HP**: 1d6 per Level; +1 after Level 10
- **Combat Aptitude**: Semi-martial
- **Armour**: Any, including shields (except
arcane or fairy magic armour)
- **Weapons**: Any (except arcane or fairy magic
weapons)
- **Additional Language**: clerics gain Liturgic, the language of Church scripture. 
- **Alignment**: Lawful or Neutral

### Starting Equipment
- Armour (1d6): 1–2. Leather  3–4. Chainmail  5. Plate mail  6. Plate mail + shield
- Weapons (roll 1d6 twice): 1–2. Mace  3. Flail  4–5. Staff  6. War hammer
- Class items: Holy symbol (silver)

### Class Abilities

**Holy Magic**: Clerics receive holy spells starting at Level 2. They may memorise spells from the holy spell list. Unlike arcane spells, holy spells do not need to be learned from books—Clerics pray for spells each day.

**DETECT HOLY MAGIC ITEMS**: A cleric can detect whether an item is enchanted with holy magic. The cleric must touch the object and
concentrate without distraction. Detecting holy magic takes 1 Turn.

**Holy Order**: At level 2 the cleric chooses a Holy Order. The Order grants the cleric an ability and specifies the holy symbol.
    - **The Order of St Faxis** 
        - **Arcane antipathy**: A cleric of St Faxis gains a +2 bonus to Saving Throws against arcane magic. Arcane spell-casters suffer a –2 penalty to Saving Throws against spells cast by a cleric of St Faxis.
        - **Holy symbol**: Three crossed swords.
    - **The Order of St Sedge**
        - **Laying on hands**: A cleric of St Sedge can heal by laying their hands on wounded characters. Once a day, the cleric can heal a total of up to 1 Hit Point per Level.
        - **Holy symbol**: A hand with two fingers raised.
    - **The Order of St Signis**
        - **Undead slayer**: A cleric of St Signis gains a +1 Attack bonus against undead monsters. Their attacks harm undead monsters that can normally only be harmed by magical or silver weapons, even when not wielding a weapon of the appropriate type.
        - **Holy symbol**: A human skull crowned with ivy.

**Turn Undead**: Once per Round as an action, the Cleric presents their holy symbol and attempts to turn undead. Roll 2d6 and consult the Turn Undead table (in the Referee's resources). Higher rolls affect more powerful undead. Turned undead flee for 1d6 Turns. On a roll of 12+, lower-level undead may be destroyed outright.

## Enchanter

- **Prime Abilities**: CHA, INT
- **HP**: 1d6 per Level; +1 after Level 10
- **Combat Aptitude**: Semi-martial
- **Armour**: Light and Medium; no shields
- **Weapons**: Small and Medium
- **Kindred Restriction**: Typically only fairies and demi-fey (elves, grimalkins, woodgrues). Mortals with a strong connection to Fairy may qualify in exceptional circumstances.

### Starting Equipment
- Armour (roll 1d6): 1–2. None  3–4. Leather  5–6. Chainmail
- Weapons (roll 1d6 twice): 1. Club  2. Dagger  3. Longsword  4. Shortbow + 20 arrows  5. Spear  6. Staff

### Class Abilities

**Detect Magic**: An enchanter can detect whether an object, place, or creature is magical. Requires touch and 1 Turn of concentration per attempt; the Referee rolls in secret so the player never knows if the roll failed or no magic is present. May retry as often as desired (1 Turn each). Given 1 hour of solitude, automatically detects magic. Skill Target improves with Level (see Enchanter Skill Targets).

**Fairy Runes**: Enchanters are granted the use of fairy runes — secret sigils guarded by the rulers of Fairy. Begins with 1 randomly selected lesser rune. Each time a Level is gained, roll for a chance of acquiring a new rune. See Fairy Magic.

**Glamours**: Knows a number of glamours determined by the Enchanter Advancement table; chosen randomly. Kindred glamours (e.g. from elf or grimalkin ancestry) are in addition to class glamours.

**Magic Items**: May use magic items restricted to arcane spell-casters (e.g. arcane scrolls, wands).

**Resistance to Divine Aid**: If the enchanter is the subject of a beneficial holy spell, there is a 2-in-6 chance it has no effect.

## Fighter

- **Prime Abilities**: STR
- **HP**: 1d8 per Level; +2 after Level 10
- **Combat Aptitude**: Martial
- **Armour**: Any, including shields
- **Weapons**: Any

### Starting Equipment
- Armour (roll 1d6): 1. Leather  2. Leather + shield  3. Chainmail  4. Chainmail + shield  5. Plate mail  6. Plate mail + shield
- Weapons (roll 1d6 twice): 1. Crossbow + 20 quarrels  2. Dagger  3. Longsword  4. Mace  5. Shortsword  6. Spear

### Class Abilities

**Combat Talents**: At Levels 2, 6, 10, and 14, the fighter rolls or chooses one talent from the list below.

1. **Battle Rage**: When in melee, enter a berserk rage lasting until end of combat. +2 to Attack and Damage Rolls; −4 AC; cannot flee.
2. **Cleave**: On a killing blow in melee, immediately make an extra attack against a second foe at −2 to hit.
3. **Defender**: While in melee with a foe, that foe's Attack Rolls against any other character are penalised at −2.
4. **Last Stand**: If reduced to 0 HP, may act for up to 5 additional Rounds before dying. Save Versus Doom each time damaged during this period or die immediately. Magical healing is effective, but saves are still required.
5. **Leader**: Mercenaries and retainers within 60′ gain +1 Morale/Loyalty. All allies within 60′ gain +2 to Saving Throws against fear effects.
6. **Main Gauche**: When fighting with a Small melee weapon in the off hand, choose each Round to gain either +1 AC or +1 to Attack Rolls.
7. **Slayer**: +1 to Attack and Damage Rolls against a specific type of foe chosen when the talent is selected (e.g. bears, crookhorns, undead, wyrms). May be chosen multiple times, each for a different foe type.
8. **Weapon Specialist**: +1 to Attack and Damage Rolls with a specific type of weapon chosen when the talent is selected (e.g. maces, longbows, two-handed swords). May be chosen multiple times, each for a different weapon type.

## Friar

- **Prime Abilities**: INT, WIS
- **HP**: 1d4 per Level; +1 after Level 10
- **Combat Aptitude**: Non-martial
- **Armour**: None
- **Weapons**: Club, dagger, holy water, oil, sling, staff, torch
- **Additional Language**: Liturgic (language of Church scripture)
- **Alignment**: Lawful or Neutral
- **Kindred Restriction**: Mortals only; fairies and demi-fey have no spiritual connection with the deities of mortals

### Starting Equipment
- Weapon (roll 1d6): 1. Club  2. Dagger  3–4. Sling + 20 stones  5–6. Staff
- Class items: Friar's habit, wooden holy symbol

### Class Abilities

**Armour of Faith**: The divine blessing of the One True God grants the friar a bonus to AC depending on their Level (see Friar Advancement table).

**Culinary Implements**: A friar can employ a frying pan, cured sausage, or ham shank as a melee weapon, dealing 1d4 damage.

**Herbalism**: A single dose of a medicinal herb is sufficient for 2 subjects when used by a friar.

**Holy Magic**: Friars pray each day to receive holy spells. Requires a holy symbol. May use magic items exclusive to holy spell-casters (e.g. magic rods, holy scrolls). See Friar Spells Per Day table.

**Poverty**: A friar's worldly possessions are limited to what can be carried on their person or mount. Excess wealth must be donated to worthy causes. The friar must dress in a simple habit and wear a traditional tonsure.

**Skills**: Skill Target of 5 for Survival when foraging.

**Turning the Undead**: Once per Turn, present holy symbol and invoke the One True God against undead within 30′. Roll 2d6: 4 or lower — unaffected; 5–6 — 2d4 undead stunned for 1 Round; 7–12 — 2d4 undead flee for 1 Turn; 13+ — 2d4 undead permanently destroyed. Modify the roll by +2 per Level the undead are below the friar (max +6), or −2 per Level they are above (max −6). In mixed groups, lowest-Level undead are affected first; on success the friar may roll again the next Round for the next type, continuing until all types are affected or a roll fails.

## Hunter

- **Prime Abilities**: CON, DEX
- **HP**: 1d8 per Level; +2 after Level 10
- **Combat Aptitude**: Martial
- **Armour**: Light, shields
- **Weapons**: Any

### Starting Equipment
- Armour (roll 1d6): 1–3. Leather  4–6. Leather + shield
- Weapons (roll 1d6 twice): 1. Dagger  2. Longsword  3–4. Longbow + 20 arrows (shortbow + 20 arrows for Small characters)  5. Shortsword  6. Sling + 20 stones

### Class Abilities

**Animal Companion**: A hunter may attempt to forge a bond with a single animal by approaching peacefully and spending 1 Turn interacting with it. On a successful Charisma Check, the animal becomes a loyal companion. Wild and domestic animals are eligible; giant or magical animals at Referee's discretion; sentient animals are not. The companion may not exceed the hunter's Level. It follows the hunter everywhere, understands basic commands, and fights to defend the hunter, never checking Morale. If it dies or is dismissed, the hunter may attempt to bond with a new animal.

**Hunter Skills**: Skill Target of 5 for Survival, plus three specialised skills: Alertness, Stalking, and Tracking. Skill Targets improve with Level (see Hunter Skill Targets).

- **Alertness**: If the hunter's party is surprised, the hunter may make an Alertness Check to act normally in the Surprise Round.
- **Stalking**: In the wilds only (not in settlements, indoors, or underground). *Hiding* — make a Stalking Check to remain undetected when only light brush is available for cover. *Sneaking* — if a Surprise Roll indicates the party has been detected, make a Stalking Check to remain undetected personally.
- **Tracking**: In the wilds only. Spend 1 Turn to find tracks in a 30′ × 30′ area; on success, know the creature type and follow the trail. May not retry in the same location. Tracks may be followed until conditions worsen, requiring a new check. Modifiers: +1 soft ground, −1 hard; +1 for groups of 10+; −2 for covered tracks; −1 per day since made; −1 per hour of rain or snow; −1 for poor light.

**Missile Attacks**: +1 Attack bonus with all missile weapons.

**Trophies**: After tracking, ambushing, or chasing down and slaying a creature, the hunter may take a sizeable trophy (at least 50 coins in weight). While the trophy is on the hunter's person or mounted in their home, the hunter gains +1 to Attack Rolls and +1 to Saving Throws against special attacks from other creatures of the same type.

**Wayfinding**: If the party becomes lost, there is a 3-in-6 chance the hunter can find the path again.

## Knight

- **Prime Abilities**: CHA, STR
- **HP**: 1d8 per Level; +2 after Level 10
- **Combat Aptitude**: Martial
- **Armour**: Medium and Heavy, shields
- **Weapons**: Any melee weapons (no missile weapons)
- **Alignment**: Must match their liege's Alignment
- **Kindred**: Typically humans and breggles only

### Starting Equipment
- Armour (roll 1d6): 1. Chainmail  2–3. Chainmail + shield  4. Plate mail  5–6. Plate mail + shield
- Weapons (roll 1d6 twice): 1. Dagger  2–4. Lance (spear for Small characters)  5. Longsword  6. Mace

### Class Abilities

**Chivalric Code**: Knights uphold a stringent code of honour in all deeds. Violating the code risks Disfavour from the knight's liege (see Liege below). The Code of Chivalry: *Honour* — a knight's honour is paramount; death before dishonour. *Service* — service to one's liege is the greatest honour. *Glory* — seek glory in battle, especially single combat. *Protecting the weak* — defend any person in the knight's charge to the death. *Hierarchy* — uphold the social order; honour superiors, respect equals, command inferiors.

**Horsemanship**: Knights are expert riders and can assess the worth of any steed (determining whether it has low, average, or high HP for its type). From Level 5, once per day, a knight may urge their steed to great speed, increasing its Speed by 10 for up to 6 Turns.

**Knighthood**: Levels 1–2 knights are squires and not yet regarded as true knights. Upon reaching Level 3, the character is knighted by their liege and gains the right to bear a coat of arms. Once knighted, the character earns rights of hospitality and aid from nobles and other knights of the same Alignment (extending to companions the knight vouches for); the knight is expected to extend such hospitality in kind.

**Liege**: A knight serves one of the lower noble houses of Dolmenwood (see Lower Houses table). Knighthood may be revoked if the knight displeases or dishonours their liege through failure, rebellion, Alignment change, or unchivalric conduct — the character becomes a fighter of equivalent Level. Regaining the liege's favour (and knighthood) may require a special quest.

**Monster Slayer (Level 5+)**: +2 bonus to Attack and Damage Rolls against Large creatures.

**Mounted Combat**: +1 Attack bonus when mounted.

**Strength of Will**: +2 bonus to Saving Throws against fairy magic and effects that cause fear.

## Thief

- **Prime Abilities**: DEX
- **HP**: 1d4 per Level; +1 after Level 10
- **Combat Aptitude**: Semi-martial
- **Armour**: Light, no shields
- **Weapons**: Small and Medium

### Starting Equipment
- Armour (roll 1d6): 1–3. None  4–6. Leather
- Weapons (roll 1d6 twice): 1. Club  2. 3 daggers  3. Longsword  4. Shortbow + 20 arrows  5. Shortsword  6. Sling + 20 stones
- Class items: Thieves' tools

### Class Abilities

**Back-Stab**: Melee dagger attack from behind against a target unaware of the thief's presence (using Stealth to hide or sneak counts as unaware). Target must be a mortal, fairy, or demi-fey of Small or Medium size. +4 Attack bonus; on hit, deals 3d4 damage (modified by STR; magic daggers add their bonus).

**Thief Skills**: Skill Target of 5 for Listen and Search, plus six specialised skills: Climb Wall, Decipher Document, Disarm Mechanism, Legerdemain, Pick Lock, and Stealth. Skill Targets improve with Level (see Thief Skill Targets).

- **Climb Wall**: Climb vertical or steep surfaces with minimal handholds, no equipment needed. Long climbs (over 100′) require a check per 100′ stretch. On a natural 1, Save Versus Doom or fall from the halfway point (1d6 per 10′). May retry (1 Turn per attempt).
- **Decipher Document**: Understand the gist of a non-magical text in an unknown language, unravel a cipher, or identify cryptically labelled landmarks. May only retry after gaining a Level.
- **Disarm Mechanism**: Disarm clockwork-like trap mechanisms in locks, lids, door handles, etc. Requires thieves' tools; 1 Turn per attempt. On a natural 1, Save Versus Doom or accidentally spring the trap. May retry (1 Turn each).
- **Legerdemain**: Pilfer a small item or perform sleight of hand (palm object, slip poison into drink). −1 penalty per 3 Levels of the victim or observer. On a natural 1, Save Versus Doom or be noticed.
- **Pick Lock**: Open a lock without a key. Requires thieves' tools; 1 Turn per attempt. Difficult locks may incur penalties or limit attempts (retry only after gaining a Level). May otherwise retry as often as desired.
- **Stealth**: *Hiding* — remain undetected when shadows are the only cover available. *Sneaking* — if a Surprise Roll indicates the party was detected, make a Stealth Check to remain undetected personally.

**Thieves' Cant**: Thieves learn a secret language of gestures and code words that allows them to hide messages in seemingly normal conversation.

## Notes on Save Target Categories

All classes have five Save Targets. Roll 1d20 + any modifiers; succeed if equal to or exceeding the target.

- **Doom**: Instantly-killing or lethal physical attacks (e.g. death rays, dragon breath)
- **Ray**: Energy rays, lightning bolts, and similar targeted effects
- **Hold**: Paralysis, petrification, falling, and physical restraint
- **Blast**: Area-of-effect attacks (fireballs, explosions, breath weapons that don't insta-kill)
- **Spell**: Potent, directly-targeted spells (arcane or holy, glamours, fairy runes)

**Wisdom Modifier** applies to all Saving Throws against magical effects.
